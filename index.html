Introduction (Phase 1)

University of New Brunswick (UNB) stands as an icon of academic excellence in the world, renowned for its unwavering commitment to scholarship, innovation, and engagements. Each year, UNB’s reputation draws a diverse group of students from all over the world, spanning locals, nationals, and internationals. Students travel thousands of kilometers to come to Fredericton, which would be their home for the next four or five years.

The concept of ‘home’ holds profound significance for UNB students rather than being a mere accommodation. This ‘home’ should be ideal with easy transport, accessible entertainment and recreational centres, and importantly closer stores. This project is going to look into this matter and help new UNB students choose where they call ‘home’ effectively.


Study Area:

Fredericton, NB

Data Processing/Analysis (Phase 4)

Initially, I filtered out the apartments and houses in Fredericton from the building outline data which contained the outlines of all building using query. Then, a 800m buffer was created around the UNB campus. Houses and Apartments within that buffer was selected and exported to a new feature class.

The image on the left shows all the houses and apartments in Fredericton. The image on the right shows the houses and apartments within 800m buffer of UNB campus. Drag the slider to see the before and after.

Additionally, a 1500m buffer from major grocery stores was created and intersected with UNB campus buffer to find an ideal location for residency. The following model from Model builder shows this process.

Having a bus stop within 100 metres of an ideal home is important for several reasons such as easier mobility, sustainable transportation practices, and alleviating traffic congestions in communities. Thus, having a bus stop near our ideal home is important. The bus stop should be within 100m of the location so that the student would not need to travel long to access the bus transportation. For this, I created a 100m buffer of every bus stations within the 800m buffer of UNB campus. Then, I selected every houses/apartments within that 100m buffer and exported it to a new feature class. The following model shows this process:

I originally intended for the parks to be within 5000m of the ideal locations. Since, almost every park in Fredericton were within that range, I did not need any additional geoprocessing for this. The trails were also connected to every parks so it was convenient.

After all the geoprocessing, approximately 380 houses/apartments were chosen as ideal place for students. The data or an excel table showing the information on these houses showing their address, property type, and more are given below. Click on the Button to access the table.

Conclusion and Links

Identifying the ideal place to call home for UNB students necessitates a careful balance of factors crucial to their academic and personal growth. From the vibrant downtown scene to the serene riverfront, Fredericton emerges as an oasis for students seeking a supportive environment that combines academic excellence with a rich social life. The proximity to campus amenities and accessible public transportation further enhances the appeal of this city, offering convenience and connectivity for students pursuing their academic endeavors. As UNB continues to attract students from diverse backgrounds, the significance of finding the perfect home in Fredericton remains paramount, not only fostering a sense of belonging but also facilitating a fulfilling university experience. This pursuit of the ideal home embodies the essence of student life at UNB, where academic pursuits intertwine with community engagement and personal growth, laying the foundation for a successful and memorable journey through higher education.
